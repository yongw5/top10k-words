    pike

      noun
    noun
    BrE BrE//paɪk//   ; NAmE NAmE//paɪk//  
    Fish
     

  1. 1 (pl. pike) a large freshwater fish with very sharp teeth
  2. 2 a weapon with a sharp blade on a long wooden handle, used in the past by soldiers on foot
  3. 3(North American English) = turnpike
  4. 4(dialect) a pointed top of a hill in the north of England the Langdale Pikes
    5.  Word Origin sense 1 Middle English: from pike ‘weapon’ (because of the fish's pointed jaw). sense 2 and sense 4 early 16th cent.: from French pique, back-formation from piquer ‘pierce’, from pic ‘pick, pike’; compare with Old English pīc ‘point, prick’ (of unknown origin). Sense (4) is apparently of Scandinavian origin; compare with West Norwegian dialect pīk ‘pointed mountain’. Idioms
    come down the pike
     

    (North American English, informal) to happen; to become noticeable We're hearing a lot about new inventions coming down the pike.
