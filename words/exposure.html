    BrE BrE//ɪkˈspəʊʒə(r)//   ; NAmE NAmE//ɪkˈspoʊʒər//  
     

    to something harmful
  1. 1[uncountable] exposure (to something) the state of being in a place or situation where there is no protection from something harmful or unpleasant prolonged exposure to harmful radiation (finance) the company’s exposure on the foreign exchange markets (= to the risk of making financial losses)
    2.  showing truth
  2. 2[uncountable] the state of having the true facts about somebody/something told after they have been hidden because they are bad, immoral or illegal his exposure as a liar and a fraud the exposure of illegal currency deals
    3.  on TV/in newspapers, etc.
  3. 3[uncountable] the fact of being discussed or mentioned on television, in newspapers, etc. synonym publicity Her new movie has had a lot of exposure in the media.
    4.  medical condition
  4. 4 [uncountable] a medical condition caused by being out in very cold weather for too long without protection Two climbers were brought in suffering from exposure.
    5.  film in camera
  5. 5[countable] a length of film in a camera that is used to take a photograph There are three exposures left on this roll of film.
  6. 6[countable] the length of time for which light is allowed to reach the film when taking a photograph I used a long exposure for this one.
    7.  showing something hidden
  7. 7[uncountable] the act of showing something that is usually hidden see also indecent exposure
