facilitate something (formal) to make an action or a process possible or easier The new trade agreement should facilitate more rapid economic growth. Structured teaching facilitates learning. Word Origin early 17th cent.: from French faciliter, from Italian facilitare, from facile ‘easy’, from Latin facilis ‘easy’, from facere ‘do, make’.Extra examples The use of computers has greatly facilitated the firm’s ability to keep accurate records. Banking secrecy laws need to be lifted in order to facilitate investigation. The smaller meetings will facilitate participation by all staff.
