id

  noun
noun
BrE BrE//ɪd//   ; NAmE NAmE//ɪd//  
(psychology)
 

the part of the unconscious mind where many of a person’s basic needs, feelings and desires are supposed to exist compare ego, superego Word Origin 1920s: from Latin, literally ‘that’, translating German es. The term was first used in this sense by Freud, following use in a similar sense by his contemporary, Georg Groddeck.
