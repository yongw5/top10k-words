  1. 1  [countable, usually singular] range (of something) a variety of things of a particular type The hotel offers a wide range of facilities. There is a full range of activities for children. This material is available in a huge range of colours.
    2.  limits
  2. 2  [countable, usually singular] the limits between which something varies Most of the students are in the 17–20 age range. There will be an increase in the range of 0 to 3 per cent. It's difficult to find a house in our price range (= that we can afford). This was outside the range of his experience.
    3.  of products
  3. 3  [countable] a set of products of a particular type our new range of hair products see also mid-range, top of the range
    4.  distance
  4. 4  [countable, uncountable] the distance over which something can be seen or heard The child was now out of her range of vision (= not near enough for her to see).
  5. 5  [countable, uncountable] the distance over which a gun or other weapon can hit things These missiles have a range of 300 miles. see also close-range, long-range, short-range
  6. 6[countable] the distance that a vehicle will travel before it needs more fuel
    7.  of mountains
  7. 7[countable] a line or group of mountains or hills the great mountain range of the Alps
    8.  for shooting
  8. 8[countable] an area of land where people can practise shooting or where bombs, etc. can be tested a shooting range A device was exploded at the main nuclear testing range. see also driving range, rifle range
    9.  oven
  9. 9[countable] a large piece of equipment that can burn various fuels and is kept hot all the time, used for cooking, especially in the past
  10. 10(North American English) = stove Cook the meat on a low heat on top of the range.
    11.  for cows
  11. 11the range [singular] (North American English) a large open area for keeping cows, etc.

    3.  arrange
  3. 3[transitive, usually passive] range somebody/something/yourself + adv./prep. (formal) to arrange people or things in a particular position or order The delegates ranged themselves around the table. Spectators were ranged along the whole route of the procession.
    4.  move around
  4. 4[intransitive, transitive] to move around an area + adv./prep. He ranges far and wide in search of inspiration for his paintings. range something Her eyes ranged the room.
