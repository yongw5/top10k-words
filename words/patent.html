BrE BrE//ˈpætnt//   , also BrE//ˈpeɪtnt//   ; NAmE NAmE//ˈpætnt//  
[countable, uncountable] an official right to be the only person to make, use or sell a product or an invention; a document that proves this to apply for/obtain a patent on an invention The device was protected by patent. Word Origin late Middle English: from Old French, from Latin patent- ‘lying open’, from the verb patere. The noun sense is from letters patent.Extra examples Edison took out a patent on the light bulb. Genetically engineered plants can be protected by patent.
