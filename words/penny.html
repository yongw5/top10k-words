    BrE BrE//pens//   ; NAmE NAmE//pens//  
    )     In senses 1 and 2, pennies is used to refer to the coins, and pence to refer to an amount of money. In sense 3, the plural is pennies.
     

  1. 1  (abbreviation p) a small British coin and unit of money. There are 100 pence in one pound (£1). He had a few pennies in his pocket. That will be 45 pence, please. They cost 20p each.
  2. 2(abbreviation d) a British coin in use until 1971. There were twelve pennies in one shilling.
  3. 3  (North American English) a cent
    4.  Word Origin Old English penig, penning of Germanic origin; related to Dutch penning, German Pfennig, perhaps also to the verb pawn and (with reference to shape) pan. Idioms
