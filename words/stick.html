  1. 1  [countable] a thin piece of wood that has fallen or been broken from a tree We collected dry sticks to start a fire. The boys were throwing sticks and stones at the dog. Her arms and legs were like sticks (= very thin).
    2.  for walking
  2. 2  [countable] (especially British English) = walking stick The old lady leant on her stick as she talked. see also shooting stick, white stick
    3.  in sport
  3. 3[countable] a long thin object that is used in some sports to hit or control the ball a hockey stick
    4.  long thin piece
  4. 4  [countable] (often in compounds) a long thin piece of something a stick of dynamite carrot sticks (North American English) a stick of butter see also French stick
  5. 5[countable] (often in compounds) a thin piece of wood or plastic that you use for a particular purpose pieces of pineapple on sticks The men were carrying spades and measuring sticks. see also chopstick, cocktail stick, drumstick, matchstick, selfie stick, yardstick
    6.  of glue, etc.
  6. 6[countable] a quantity of a substance, such as solid glue, that is sold in a small container with round ends and straight sides, and can be pushed further out of the container as it is used see also lipstick
    7.  in plane/vehicle
  7. 7[countable] (informal, especially North American English) the control stick of a plane see also joystick
  8. 8[countable] (informal, especially North American English) a handle used to change the gears of a vehicle see also gear lever, stick shift
    9.  for orchestra
  9. 9[countable] a baton, used by the person who conducts an orchestra

  11. 11the sticks [plural] (informal, usually disapproving) country areas, a long way from cities We live out in the sticks.
