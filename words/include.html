  1. 1  (not used in the progressive tenses) if one thing includes another, it has the second thing as one of its parts include something The tour included a visit to the Science Museum. Does the price include tax? include doing something Your duties include typing letters and answering the telephone.
  2. 2  to make somebody/something part of something include somebody/something (as/in/on something) You should include some examples in your essay. We all went, me included. include somebody/something as something Representatives from the country were included as observers at the conference. opposite exclude
    3.  Word Origin late Middle English (also in the sense ‘shut in’): from Latin includere, from in- ‘into’ + claudere ‘to shut’.Extra examples Helpers at the event included students from a local school, as well as their parents. Quite a few members are worried and I include myself among them. We all went, me included. Your duties will include typing letters and answering the phone.
