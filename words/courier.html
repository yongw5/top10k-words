  1. 1a person or company whose job is to take packages or important papers somewhere We sent the documents by courier. I’ll send a courier with the blueprints. Urgent deliveries of medicine may be sent by motorcycle courier.
  2. 2(British English) a person who is employed by a travel company to give advice and help to a group of tourists on holiday
    3. Word Origin late Middle English (denoting a person sent to run with a message): originally from Old French coreor; later from French courier (now courrier), from Italian corriere; based on Latin currere ‘to run’.
