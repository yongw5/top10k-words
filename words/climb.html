    go up
  1. 1  [transitive, intransitive] climb (up) (something) to go up something towards the top to climb a mountain/hill/tree/wall She climbed up the stairs. The car slowly climbed the hill. As they climbed higher, the air became cooler.
    2. go through/down/over
  2. 2  [intransitive] + adv./prep. to move somewhere, especially with difficulty or effort I climbed through the window. Sue climbed into bed. Can you climb down? The boys climbed over the wall.
    3.  mountain/rock, etc.
  3. 3  go climbing to go up mountains or climb rocks as a hobby or sport He likes to go climbing most weekends.
    4.  aircraft/sun, etc.
  4. 4  [intransitive] to go higher in the sky The plane climbed to 33 000 feet. The sun climbed higher in the sky.
    5.  slope up
  5. 5  [intransitive] to slope upwards From here the path climbs steeply to the summit.
    6.  of plants
  6. 6[intransitive] to grow up a wall or frame a climbing rose
    7.  increase
  7. 7  [intransitive] (of temperature, a country’s money, etc.) to increase in value or amount The dollar has been climbing all week. The paper's circulation continues to climb. Membership is climbing steadily.
    8. improve position/status
  8. 8[intransitive] climb (to something) to move to a higher position or social rank by your own effort In a few years he had climbed to the top of his profession. The team has now climbed to fourth in the league.
