    as good as
     

    very nearly The matter is as good as settled. He as good as called me a coward (= suggested that I was a coward without actually using the word ‘coward’). She’s as good as won.
    as good as it gets
     

    used when you are saying that a situation is not going to get any better
    be good to go
     

    (of a thing) to be prepared and ready for use; (of a person) to be prepared and ready to do something By tomorrow afternoon the document will be good to go. I’ve spent several months training for this race so now I’m good to go.
    good and…
     

    (informal) completely I won't go until I'm good and ready.
    a good few
     

    several There are still a good few empty seats.
    good for you, somebody, them, etc. (especially Australian English good on you, etc.)
     

    (informal) used to praise somebody for doing something well ‘I passed first time.’ ‘Good for you!’
