MAD

 
BrE   ; NAmE  
(also MAD Magazine)
 

a US humorous magazine, first published in 1952. It uses an exaggerated cartoon style to make fun of films, advertisements, etc., and to make well-known people look foolish. It usually has on its cover a picture of the imaginary character Alfred E Neuman, who has a wide face and a stupid smile and says ‘What, me worry?’

    (informal) (of a person) mentally ill; very silly Because of the chemicals used in hat-making, workers often suffered from mercury poisoning, which can cause loss of memory and damage to the nervous system. Lewis Carroll was probably thinking of this when he created the eccentric character of the Hatter in Alice’s Adventures in Wonderland. A March hare was called mad because of the strange behaviour of hares during the mating season.
