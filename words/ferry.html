Travelling by boat or ship
 

[transitive, intransitive] ferry (somebody/something) (+ adv./prep.) to carry people or goods in a boat or other vehicle from one place to another, often for a short distance and as a regular service He offered to ferry us across the river in his boat. The children need to be ferried to and from school. You really don’t have to ferry us around while we’re in town. Word Origin Middle English: from Old Norse ferja ‘ferry boat’, of Germanic origin and related to fare.
