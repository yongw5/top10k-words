    organize
  1. 1  [transitive] mount something to organize and begin something synonym arrange to mount a protest/campaign/an exhibition
    2.  increase
  2. 2  [intransitive] to increase gradually Pressure is mounting on the government to change the law. The death toll continues to mount. see also mounting
    3.  go up something
  3. 3  [transitive] mount something (formal) to go up something, or up on to something that is raised synonym ascend She slowly mounted the steps. He mounted the platform and addressed the crowd.
    4.  bicycle/horse
  4. 4  [transitive, intransitive] mount (something) (rather formal) to get on a bicycle, horse, etc. in order to ride it He mounted his horse and rode away. opposite dismount see also mounted
    5. picture/jewel, etc.
  5. 5[transitive] mount something (on/onto/in something) to fix something into position on something, so that you can use it, look at it or study it The specimens were mounted on slides. The diamond is mounted in gold.
    6.  of male animal
  6. 6[transitive] mount something to get onto the back of a female animal in order to have sex
