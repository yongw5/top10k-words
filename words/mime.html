mime

  noun
noun

Types of play
 

(less frequent dumbshow) [uncountable, countable] (especially in the theatre) the use of movements of your hands or body and the expressions on your face to tell a story or to act something without speaking; a performance using this method of acting The performance consisted of dance, music and mime. a mime artist She performed a brief mime. Word Origin early 17th cent. (also in the sense ‘mimic or jester’): from Latin mimus, from Greek mimos.
