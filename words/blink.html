  1. 1[intransitive, transitive] blink (something) when you blink or blink your eyes or your eyes blink, you shut and open your eyes quickly He blinked in the bright sunlight. Lucy blinked at him in astonishment. I'll be back before you can blink (= very quickly). When I told him the news he didn't even blink (= showed no surprise at all). compare wink
  2. 2[intransitive] to shine with an unsteady light; to flash on and off Suddenly a warning light blinked.
    3.  Word Origin Middle English: from blenk, Scots variant of blench, reinforced by Middle Dutch blinken ‘to shine’. Early senses included ‘deceive’, ‘flinch’ (compare with blench), and also ‘open the eyes after sleep’: hence sense (1) (mid 16th cent.).Extra examples He blinked in surprise.
