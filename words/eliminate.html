  1. 1to remove or get rid of something/somebody eliminate something/somebody Credit cards eliminate the need to carry a lot of cash. eliminate something/somebody from something The police have eliminated two suspects from their investigation. This diet claims to eliminate toxins from the body.
  2. 2eliminate somebody (from something) [usually passive] to defeat a person or a team so that they no longer take part in a competition, etc. synonym knock out All the English teams were eliminated in the early stages of the competition. She was eliminated from the tournament in the first round.
  3. 3eliminate somebody (formal) to kill somebody, especially an enemy or opponent Most of the regime's left-wing opponents were eliminated.
