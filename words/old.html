In British English you can also use elder and eldest when comparing the ages of people, especially members of the same family, although these words are not common in speech now. As adjectives they are only used before a noun and you cannot say ‘elder than’:my older/​elder sister the elder/​older of their two children I’m the eldest/​oldest in the family.
