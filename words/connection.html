    3.  in electrical system
  3. 3  [countable] a point, especially in an electrical system, where two parts connect A faulty connection caused the machine to stop.
    4.  train/bus/plane
  4. 4  [countable] a train, bus or plane at a station or an airport that a passenger can take soon after getting off another in order to continue their journey We arrived in good time for the connection to Paris. I missed the connection by five minutes. Wordfinderaisle, buffet, carriage, connection, locomotive, luggage rack, platform, station, track, train
  5. 5[countable, usually plural] a means of travelling to another place There are good bus and train connections between the resort and major cities.
    6.  person/organization
  6. 6[countable, usually plural] a person or an organization that you know and that can help or advise you in your social or professional life synonym contact One of my business connections gave them my name.
    7.  distant relatives
  7. 7connections [plural] people who are your relatives, but not members of your close family She is British but also has German connections. a network of family connections in Italy
