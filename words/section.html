    section

      noun
    noun
    BrE BrE//ˈsekʃn//   ; NAmE NAmE//ˈsekʃn//  
     

    part/piece
  1. 1  [countable] any of the parts into which something is divided That section of the road is still closed. The library has a large biology section. the tail section of the plane
  2. 2  [countable] a separate part of a structure from which the whole can be put together The shed comes in sections that you assemble yourself.
    3.  of document/book
  3. 3  [countable] a separate part of a document, book, etc. These issues will be discussed more fully in the next section. the sports section of the newspaper The report has a section on accidents at work.
    4.  group of people
  4. 4  [countable] a separate group within a larger group of people an issue that will affect large sections of the population the brass section of an orchestra see also rhythm section
    5.  of organization
  5. 5[countable] a department in an organization, institution, etc. synonym division He's the director of the finance section. the section of the company dealing with customer services
    6.  district
  6. 6[countable] (North American English) a district of a town, city or county the Dorchester section of Boston one of the city’s most affluent sections
    7.  measurement
  7. 7[countable] (North American English) a measure of land, equal to one square mile
    8.  diagram
  8. 8[countable] a drawing or diagram of something as it would look if it were cut from top to bottom or from one side to the other The illustration shows a section through a leaf. The architect drew the house in section. see also cross section
    9.  medical
  9. 9 [countable, uncountable] (medical) the act of cutting or separating something in an operation The surgeon performed a section (= made a cut) on the vein. see also caesarean
  10. 10 [countable] (medical, biology) a very thin flat piece cut from body tissue to be looked at under a microscope to examine a section from the kidney
    11.  Word Origin late Middle English (as a noun): from French section or Latin sectio(n-), from secare ‘to cut’.     3.  mental patient
  3. 3[often passive] section somebody (British English) to officially order a mentally ill person to go and receive treatment in a psychiatric hospital, using a law that can force them to stay there until they are successfully treated
