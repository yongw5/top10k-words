  1. 1[transitive] foster something to encourage something to develop synonym encourage, promote The club's aim is to foster better relations within the community.
  2. 2[transitive, intransitive] foster (somebody) (especially British English) to take another person’s child into your home for a period of time, without becoming his or her legal parents They have fostered over 60 children during the past ten years. We couldn't adopt a child, so we decided to foster.
