    reflection

      noun
    (also British English, old-fashioned reflexion) noun
    BrE BrE//rɪˈflekʃn//   ; NAmE NAmE//rɪˈflekʃn//  
     

  1. 1[countable] an image in a mirror, on a shiny surface, on water, etc. He admired his reflection in the mirror.
  2. 2[uncountable] the action or process of sending back light, heat, sound, etc. from a surface
  3. 3[countable] a sign that shows the state or nature of something Your clothes are often a reflection of your personality. The increase in crime is a sad reflection on (= shows something bad about) our society today.
  4. 4[uncountable] careful thought about something, sometimes over a long period of time She decided on reflection to accept his offer after all. A week off would give him time for reflection.
  5. 5[countable, usually plural] your written or spoken thoughts about a particular subject or topic a book of her reflections on childhood
  6. 6[countable] an account or a description of something The article is an accurate reflection of events that day.
    7.
